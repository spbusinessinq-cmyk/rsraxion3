Redesign the interface into an operator-level intelligence console.

The current AXION UI still feels too soft and app-like.

VISUAL RULES:

1. Remove almost all purple.
   - Purple may only remain as a tiny brand accent around the AXION wordmark or rare micro-highlight.
   - No purple panels.
   - No purple gradient fills.
   - No glowing purple buttons.

2. Base colors:
   - near-black
   - matte black
   - charcoal
   - gunmetal
   - steel gray

3. Accent colors:
   - emerald green = active / verified / connected
   - amber = elevated / watch / caution
   - red = critical / failed / urgent
   - cyan = rare data highlight only

4. Typography:
   - Orbitron for headings, system labels, console titles, major stats
   - IBM Plex Mono or similar monospace for data, timestamps, labels, logs, archive entries
   - clean sans-serif only if needed for long readable body text

5. Layout:
   - hard grid
   - thin 1px borders
   - squared or very slightly rounded corners only
   - no bubbly cards
   - no SaaS dashboard look
   - panels should feel embedded into a system, not floating

6. Motion:
   - minimal
   - fast
   - no bounce
   - no playful transitions
   - subtle fade only

7. Background:
   - near-black
   - optional faint orbital grid / world map / satellite-line texture at extremely low opacity
   - professional orbital command atmosphere
   - not sci-fi gimmick
   - not cyberpunk

Target feeling:

“If this UI was on a screen in a classified facility, nobody would question it.”

PART 5 — SIGNAL PROCESSING ENGINE v3
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Upgrade AXION’s signal processing logic.

For every signal:

1. Clean the signal
   - remove weak phrasing
   - normalize title
   - remove noise
   - strip irrelevant entertainment/sports/lifestyle unless directly tied to policy, security, technology, markets, or infrastructure

2. Deduplicate
   - merge overlapping signals
   - keep strongest source/title
   - preserve relevant domain diversity

3. Classify confidence:
   - CONFIRMED
   - LIKELY
   - CONTESTED
   - UNKNOWN

4. Score signal weight:
   - 0–100
   - based on severity, relevance, institutional impact, domain coupling, recency, and source confidence

5. Assign domain(s)

6. Assign threat level:
   - LOW
   - GUARDED
   - ELEVATED
   - HIGH
   - CRITICAL

7. Identify whether it is:
   - isolated
   - reinforcing
   - converging
   - escalating
   - stabilizing
   - fragmenting

PART 6 — PRESSURE MODEL
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Add a pressure model to the brief logic and UI.

AXION should identify:

1. Pressure Building
   - where stress is accumulating

2. Pressure Releasing
   - where risk is cooling or being absorbed

3. Pressure Transferring
   - where pressure is moving from one domain to another

Examples:
- energy pressure transferring into inflation
- conflict pressure transferring into shipping and supply chains
- technology policy pressure transferring into compute infrastructure
- governance pressure transferring into legal or institutional domains

This must appear in full briefs and daily/weekly reports.

Explain the broader meaning.
Measured, analytical, controlled.

9. CONSTRAINTS

- political resistance

- market response
- institutional limits

10. FORWARD PROJECTION

- Failure Path

11. OPERATOR TAKEAWAY
Short.
Direct.
What the reader should walk away understanding.

Specific triggers to monitor next.

13. APPENDIX / SUPPORTING SIGNALS
Compact list of additional signals not used as primary drivers.
